# Amalia Ulman

## 1. Basic facts
- Amalia Ulman, born 1989 in Argentina, is an Argentine-Spanish artist working across performance, photography, installation, video, social media, and digital image circulation [S1][S2].
- She is best known in photography and media-art history for `Excellences & Perfections` (2014), a scripted durational performance conducted through Instagram and Facebook and later archived by Rhizome / the New Museum [S2][S3].
- Her work is relevant to photography history because it treats the social-media feed, selfie, caption, follower reaction, and archival screenshot as parts of a photographic performance system [S2][S3][S4].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: online self-fashioning, femininity as performance, class aspiration, consumer taste, authenticity, the staged nature of social-media photography, and the way platforms reward recognizable identities [S2][S4][S5].
- Technique / formal traits: `Excellences & Perfections` unfolded as a sequence of social-media posts rather than as a conventional photographic series. Ulman used selfies, hotel and restaurant settings, interiors, beauty imagery, captions, hashtags, timing, and audience response to build a believable persona inside the ordinary grammar of Instagram [S2][S4].
- The performance was not simply a set of images uploaded online. Rhizome describes the work as scripted and notes that its Instagram portion was captured with a social-media archiving tool, making preservation, interface, and platform context part of the artwork's afterlife [S2][S3].
- Why this method was chosen: the work emerged when Instagram was becoming a major space for everyday photographic performance. Ulman adopted the very conventions of lifestyle posting, body display, and aspirational branding that the platform normalized, turning them into a controlled fiction [S2][S4].
- Historical context: in the 2010s, smartphone photography and social media shifted the public presentation of identity from occasional portraiture to continuous image management. `Excellences & Perfections` matters because it made this condition visible from inside the platform rather than from outside it [S2][S4][S5].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: the work belongs to post-internet art, feminist performance, and performance-for-camera traditions. It also updates earlier photographic performances of femininity and constructed identity by placing the work in a live, networked feedback loop [S1][S2][S5].
- Representative works / examples: `Excellences & Perfections` (2014); related performances and projects include `Privilege` (2015-16), `The Annals of Private History`, `Shanghai Fire`, and the film `El Planeta` [S4][S6].
- Historical significance: Ulman's importance is not that she used Instagram before anyone else, but that she made the platform's belief in authenticity, audience participation, and self-branding into the material of the work. The audience's reactions were not outside reception; they were part of the performance's operation [S2][S4][S5].
- Critical meaning: the project asks whether a photograph on social media is evidence of a life, a performance of a type, or a commodity-like image shaped by class and gender codes. It also shows how photographic credibility can be produced by repetition, captioning, interface familiarity, and viewer expectation [S2][S4].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- Rhizome and the New Museum presented `Excellences & Perfections` in `First Look` in 2014 and archived the Instagram portion, giving the work an early institutional frame within net art and preservation discourse [S2][S3].
- Zabludowicz Collection notes that works documenting and responding to the performance, including audience reactions, have been exhibited internationally, including in Tate Modern's `Performing for the Camera` in 2016 [S1].
- Interview Magazine reports that Ulman discussed the project on an Art Basel Miami Beach panel titled `Instagram as an Artistic Medium`, where her scripted persona challenged the panel's assumptions about Instagram authenticity [S4].
- The project was later published as the book `Excellences & Perfections` by Prestel in 2018, with the sequence presented as an uninterrupted stream and framed as a major statement about identity, gender, class, sexuality, and lifestyle imagery [S5].

## 4. Artist statement / artist's own words
- In Interview Magazine, Ulman states that viewers' anger at the fiction of `Excellences & Perfections` revealed how strongly people still attach truth-value to online self-presentation [S4].
- In Vogue Italia, she describes her broader practice as shaped by appearances, class, empathy, acting, taste, and the challenge of inhabiting roles she does not already like or identify with [S6].

## 5. Quote candidates
- "everyone is selecting, or even fabricating, what they post" [S4].
- "most of my works have dealt with appearances, class and empathy" [S6].
- "a scripted online performance" [S2].
