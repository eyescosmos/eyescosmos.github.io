# Viviane Sassen

## 1. Basic facts
- Viviane Sassen, born 1972 in Amsterdam, is a Dutch photographer and artist working across fine art, fashion photography, collage, painting, and video [S1][S2].
- She studied fashion, photography, and fine art and graduated from the Royal Academy of Arnhem in 1997 [S1].
- ICP awarded her the 2011 Infinity Award for Applied/Fashion/Advertising Photography, and MoMA included her in `New Photography 2011` [S1][S3].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: body, shadow, color, memory, Africa and Europe, displacement, intimacy, fashion, desire, sexuality, death, and the tension between seduction and disturbance [S1][S2][S3][S4].
- Technique / formal traits: Sassen uses saturated color, deep shadows, unusual perspectives, fragmented bodies, masked or obscured faces, strong geometries, and painterly or collage-based interventions [S2][S4][S5].
- Foam's `PHOSPHOR: Art & Fashion` retrospective identifies two major threads: a search for new photographic forms and the importance of intimacy. It also notes her use of photography, collage, painting, and video over more than thirty years [S2].
- Why this method was chosen: Sassen's childhood in Kenya and later return trips to Africa shape her work's recurring sense of memory, displacement, and dream. MoMA connects `Parasomnia` to mysterious remnants of memory and anonymous subjects in intentionally unidentified African countries [S3][S6].
- Historical context: Sassen's practice develops at the intersection of 2000s fashion photography, fine-art photography, postcolonial questions of gaze and memory, and museum recognition of hybrid photographic practice [S1][S3].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: `New Photography 2011` placed her alongside Moyra Davey, George Georgiou, Deana Lawson, Doug Rickard, and Zhang Dali, emphasizing diverse contemporary approaches to global image-making [S6].
- Representative works / examples: `Flamboya` (2003-06), `Parasomnia`, `Testament` (2011), `In and Out of Fashion`, `Umbra`, `Roxane`, `Of Mud and Lotus`, `Hot Mirror`, and `PHOSPHOR: Art & Fashion 1990-2023` [S1][S2][S3][S7].
- Historical significance: Sassen is important because she collapsed the boundary between fashion and art photography while maintaining a rigorous photographic language of shadow, form, and ambiguity [S1][S2][S4].
- Critical meaning: her work withholds stable identity by hiding faces, fragmenting bodies, and using shadow. This makes viewers confront the image as projection, fantasy, and archetype rather than straightforward portraiture [S4].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- ICP's Infinity Award profile documents her movement between artistic work and fashion assignments and notes her international exhibition history, African photographic work, and 2007 Prix de Rome [S1].
- MoMA's `New Photography 2011` page connects `Parasomnia` to Kenya, displacement, and dreamlike memories [S3].
- Foam's `PHOSPHOR` exhibition positions Sassen's career across art and fashion and highlights the search for new photographic forms [S2].
- National Galleries of Scotland emphasizes her experimental image-making and her aim to confuse viewers through unusual perspectives and dramatic angles [S5].
- TIME's 2011 essay describes her use of shadows, color, place, and body as sculpture, and includes the artist's explanation that she wants beauty and disturbance to coexist [S4].

## 4. Artist statement / artist's own words
- MoMA quotes Sassen on not feeling she belonged in Europe while knowing she was a foreigner in Africa, a crucial statement for interpreting displacement in `Parasomnia` [S3].
- TIME quotes Sassen on obscuring faces because she is interested less in an individual person than in a more universal idea or archetype [S4].

## 5. Quote candidates
- "I didn't feel like I belonged in Europe" [S3].
- "leave something disturbing" [S4].
- "new photographic forms" [S2].
