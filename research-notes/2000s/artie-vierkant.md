# Artie Vierkant

## 1. Basic facts
- Artie Vierkant, born 1986, is a United States-based artist working across photography, sculpture, digital files, websites, and online circulation [S1][S2].
- He received a BA in Photography from the University of Pennsylvania in 2009 and an MFA from University of California San Diego in 2011 [S1].
- His key early project is `Image Objects`, begun in 2011, developed alongside his 2010 essay `The Image Object Post-Internet` [S2][S3].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: the instability of the photographic object, the movement of images between gallery space and networked circulation, authorship after digital reproduction, and the status of documentation as artwork [S2][S3].
- Technique / formal traits: `Image Objects` begin as digital files, are fabricated as UV prints on machine-cut panels such as Sintra or aluminum composite material, and are precision-cut so that a flat photographic surface appears to carry sculptural depth [S1][S2].
- Vierkant then treats documentation not as a neutral record but as a new work. Official installation views are digitally altered by the artist; each documentation image can become a distinct version, rather than a secondary copy of a stable object [S1][S2].
- Why this method was chosen: the project responds to a historical moment in which exhibitions were increasingly experienced through blogs, smartphones, social media, and online documentation rather than only in physical galleries [S2].
- Historical context: Rhizome places the project within the early post-internet discussion, after the rise of platforms such as VVORK and Contemporary Art Daily and amid a broader collapse of the boundary between physical artwork and its online image [S2].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: the work is central to post-internet art because it does not simply use the Internet as a subject; it makes the networked reproduction, manipulation, and social recirculation of images part of the artwork's structure [S2][S4].
- Representative works / examples: `Image Objects` at Reference Gallery in 2011 and Higher Pictures in 2012; related projects listed in Vierkant's archive include `Possible Objects`, `Similar Objects`, `Browwwsing / Browwwser`, and `Exposure Adjustment on a Sunset` [S1][S3].
- Historical significance: Vierkant's contribution is useful for photography history because it reframes the photographic print as one state in a chain of digital file, object, installation image, altered documentation, and online derivative [S2].
- Critical meaning: the work challenges the idea that the photograph's value lies in indexical relation to an original object. Instead, it treats mutability, citation, and distributed viewership as conditions of contemporary image-making [S2][S4].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- Higher Pictures described the 2012 New York exhibition as Vierkant's first solo exhibition in New York and presented `Image Objects` as "at the forefront of new forms of visual art" [S1].
- Rhizome's Net Art Anthology presents `Image Objects` as a body of sculptural works between physical object and mediated image that helped shape the emerging discussion around post-internet art [S2].
- Artuner reads the project through a shift from physical representation to Internet representation and emphasizes the social relations and citations generated by the artwork [S4].
- The work has been discussed in the context of ICP's `What Is a Photograph?`-type debates over image, object, and documentation; a related report quotes Vierkant on the primary experience of an object and the secondary experience through documentation [S5].

## 4. Artist statement / artist's own words
- Vierkant's essay `The Image Object Post-Internet` is a primary theoretical source and should be treated as the main artist statement for this note [S3].
- Rhizome reproduces a short statement from the essay: "nothing is in a fixed state" [S2].

## 5. Quote candidates
- "nothing is in a fixed state" [S2].
- "the documentation becomes a separate work in itself" [S5].
- "lack of fixity in representational strategy" [S4].
