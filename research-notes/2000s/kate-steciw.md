# Kate Steciw

## 1. Basic facts
- Kate Steciw, born 1978 in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania, is a United States-based artist working across photography, sculpture, collage, video, and image manipulation [S1][S2].
- She received an MFA in Photography from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and has exhibited with Higher Pictures and other galleries [S1][S3].
- Her work is often discussed in relation to post-internet photography and the transformation of online or stock imagery into physical image-objects [S2][S4].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: the circulation of stock images, the objecthood of photographs, Web 2.0 image consumption, the commercial image database, and the translation of digital fragments into sculptural prints [S2][S4][S5].
- Technique / formal traits: Steciw sources images from the Internet or stock-image databases, cuts, layers, and modifies them digitally, then produces printed and sculptural constructions using frames, tinted Plexiglas, collage elements, and other physical supports [S2][S4][S5].
- Her titles often preserve search terms or textual traces of the image database, making the route of image retrieval part of the work's structure [S4][S5].
- Why this method was chosen: Steciw's background in commercial photo retouching is relevant because the work emerges from direct experience with the polished image economy of online commerce, advertising, and search [S5].
- Historical context: the work belongs to a 2010s shift in which artists treated the Internet not merely as a distribution channel but as the everyday condition under which images are found, consumed, altered, and made into objects [S4][S6].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: Steciw is part of a post-internet photography context that includes artists converting online image flows into prints, objects, installations, and hybrid forms; MoMA's `Ocean of Images` provides a related institutional frame for this image-saturated condition [S6].
- Representative works / examples: Higher Pictures exhibition `Kate Steciw` (2014), works titled with long search-keyword chains, and mixed-media photo constructions with Plexiglas overlays and colored frames [S1][S2][S5].
- Historical significance: Steciw's practice is useful for photography history because it makes visible how the 2000s/2010s stock-image economy changed what counted as photographic source material, authorship, and composition [S4][S5].
- Critical meaning: rather than treating found Internet images as transparent content, she turns them into unstable, tactile, and abstract structures, showing how digital images gain new meaning when cut, printed, framed, and obstructed [S2][S4].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- The New Yorker described Steciw as part of a new generation taking liberties with photography and emphasized her transformation of Internet images into layered constructions [S2].
- Artspace frames her practice as exploring the relationship between image, object, and digital representation, especially through sourced imagery and physical transformation [S4].
- Hyperallergic reads her work through the systems of Internet databanks and commercial image circulation, connecting the work to the visual logic of daily online consumption [S5].
- The New Yorker feature on `Attachments` placed Steciw within a group show about digital image making and viewing, emphasizing how many viewers encounter exhibitions through online attachments [S7].
