# Natalie Czech

## 1. Basic facts
- Natalie Czech, born 1976 in Neuss, Germany, is a Berlin-based artist working primarily with conceptual photography, text, poetry, and found printed matter [S1][S2].
- Her ongoing series `Hidden Poems` began around 2010 and is central to her reception [S1][S3].
- In 2010 she received the Alfried Krupp von Bohlen und Halbach Award for Contemporary Photography for `Hidden Poems` [S4].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: the relation between photography and poetry, reading and seeing, hidden text, authorship, quotation, found material, and the photographic transformation of printed pages [S1][S2][S3].
- Technique / formal traits: Czech finds existing poems inside magazine articles, newspapers, product packaging, album covers, reviews, and other printed sources. She marks individual letters or words with highlighters, pens, erasure, or other interventions, then photographs the marked source as a finished picture [S1][S2][S3].
- In `Hidden Poems`, texts by writers such as Robert Creeley, Rolf Dieter Brinkmann, e.e. cummings, Jack Kerouac, Robert Lax, Frank O'Hara, and others are extracted from unrelated prose or visual material [S1][S2].
- Why this method was chosen: Czech uses photography to make reading visible. The photograph is not simply a reproduction of a page; it becomes a field where linguistic meaning, material surface, scale, and viewing attention are reorganized [S1][S2].
- Historical context: her work appears in a 2010s context in which conceptual photography increasingly dealt with archives, reproduction, text-image relations, and the printed matter of cultural memory rather than conventional documentary subject matter [S2][S5].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: MAMCO frames her work at the confluence of Concrete poetry and appropriationist photography, two histories that both question what a text or image can show and tell [S3].
- Representative works / examples: `A hidden poem by Robert Lax` (2010), `A hidden poem by Rolf Dieter Brinkmann` (2010), `A hidden poem by Sarah Riggs` (2012), `Poems by Repetition` (2013), `Voyelles` (2013), and `A Critic's Bouquet` (2015) [S1][S2][S6].
- Historical significance: Czech's work is important for photography history because it treats the camera as a reading machine, turning already existing pages into new pictures that preserve traces of their source while shifting the act of interpretation [S1][S3].
- Critical meaning: the work asks viewers to move repeatedly between text and image, source and extraction, fact and interpretation. Its conceptual force lies in making the act of looking overlap with the act of reading [S1][S2].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- Frieze's 2011 review described Czech's method as finding poems in random magazine articles, emphasizing the equal partnership of poem and photograph [S1].
- Frieze's 2013 feature explains that the hidden relationships are not occult messages but ways of bringing out poetic constructions in ordinary prose [S2].
- MAMCO connects her practice to Concrete poetry and appropriationist photography, a strong art-historical frame for the work [S3].
- e-flux's announcement for `Hidden Poems` foregrounds authorship, subjectivity, documentation, and contextualization as central questions [S4].
- Zabludowicz Collection describes her process as creating layered palimpsests in which archival imagery and revealed poems enter dialogue [S5].

## 4. Artist statement / artist's own words
- Frieze cites a 2012 interview in which Czech explains that hidden relationships are not secret messages but forms of reading that reveal poetic constructions in everyday prose [S2].
