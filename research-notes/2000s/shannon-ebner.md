# Shannon Ebner

## 1. Basic facts
- Shannon Ebner, born 1971, is an American artist based in Los Angeles whose work joins photography, poetry, language, signs, and constructed alphabets [S1][S2].
- MoMA holds works including `USA` (2003), `ETC` (2010), and `PHOTORAMA` (2017), and included her in exhibitions such as `Ecstatic Alphabets/Heaps of Language` [S2].
- Her key project `The Electric Comma` was shown across the Hammer Museum, LAXART, and the 54th Venice Biennale context in 2011 [S1].

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: photography and language, alphabetic systems, political speech, poetry, delay, blankness, signal, illegibility, and the instability of meaning [S1][S3].
- Technique / formal traits: Ebner builds letters and phrases from vernacular materials such as cardboard, wood, and cinder blocks, photographs them in studios and landscapes, and also photographs found marks that behave like language [S1].
- Her works often replace letters with symbols, asterisks, backslashes, or gaps, interrupting legibility and making viewers experience language as a visual structure [S1][S4].
- `The Electric Comma` began as a poem about conditions of photography, including its alleged static nature and its relation to the past. Ebner calls the poem a "photographic sentence" [S1].
- Why this method was chosen: Ebner uses photography to push language outside its usual function. The image becomes a way to test whether a word can perform, delay, fragment, or transmit meaning differently [S1][S3].
- Historical context: her practice extends Conceptual art, concrete poetry, Ed Ruscha-like word/image work, and 1960s/70s language-based art into a 2000s photographic context [S5][S6].
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: MoMA's `Ecstatic Alphabets/Heaps of Language` places her in a broader history of experimental alphabets and language as visual material [S2].
- Representative works / examples: `Dead Democracy Letters`, `USA` (2003), `STRIKE` alphabet, `The Sun as Error` (2009), `ETC` (2010), `The Electric Comma` (2011), `EKS` (2011), and `PHOTORAMA` (2017) [S1][S2][S3][S5].
- Historical significance: Ebner is significant because she treats photography not as illustration of text but as a system that can build, interrupt, and spatialize language [S1][S3].
- Critical meaning: her work asks whether photography can make language think differently. It exposes the architecture of communication and the fragility of words when they become signs, objects, and images [S1][S4].

## 3. Criticism and reception
- Hammer Museum's exhibition text is the major source for `The Electric Comma`, emphasizing the work's relation to photography, poetry, information theory, and communication [S1].
- MoMA's artist page confirms works in the collection and exhibition contexts including `Ecstatic Alphabets/Heaps of Language` [S2].
- LACMA launched `The Sun as Error` as the first artist book published with its Wallis Annenberg Department of Photography [S3].
- Art in America's review of the Hammer project described Ebner as materializing language through sentencelike photographic logic [S4].
- Frieze's earlier review of `Dead Democracy Letters` connects Ebner's landscape-letter works to political speech, Conceptual photography, Ed Ruscha, Bruce Nauman, and Robert Smithson [S5].

## 4. Artist statement / artist's own words
