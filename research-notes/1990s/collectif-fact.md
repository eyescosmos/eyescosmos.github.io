# Collectif_fact

## 1. Basic facts
- Swiss artist collective active from the 2000s, associated with image research across photography, digital simulation, video, and installation.[S1][S2]
- Their projects are often framed through urban space, mediated vision, and the circulation of images rather than through single-authored documentary photography.[S1][S2]

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: urban fictions, image circulation, simulation, architecture, and the unstable relation between observed reality and constructed visual worlds.[S1][S2]
- Technique / formal traits: photography is combined with CGI, modeling, installation, and moving-image strategies; images often look documentary at first glance but are reorganized into constructed environments or speculative visual systems.[S1][S2]
- Representative examples: exhibition materials around projects such as *White Shadow* and other city-oriented installations are useful because they show the group treating urban imagery as something to be recomposed rather than neutrally recorded.[S1][S2]
- Why this method was chosen: the collective’s method appears designed to test how contemporary vision is already shaped by screens, architecture, and virtual interfaces. The work does not simply document the city; it studies how the city is preformatted by images.[S1][S2]
- Historical context: Collectif_fact belongs to a post-1990 field in which photography increasingly intersects with digital modeling, image databases, and installation practice. Their work sits in a moment when the authority of the photographic image is being rethought through new media.[S1][S2]
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: the work can be placed near post-conceptual photography and media-based urban research, where the image becomes part of a broader spatial and informational system.[S1][S2]
- Historical significance: the collective matters because it shows how photography after the documentary turn could be folded into synthetic and architectural image production without disappearing as a critical reference point.[S1][S2]
- Critical meaning: their work treats photography less as a final object than as one layer within an ecology of mediated seeing. Final writing should emphasize this shift from image-as-record to image-as-operation.[S1][S2]

## 3. Criticism and reception
- The available reception is mostly exhibition-based, but it consistently frames the collective around contemporary urban image culture and hybrid visual production.[S1][S2]
- Final prose should avoid attributing a single stable manifesto to the group; the safer emphasis is on how their work destabilizes the boundary between documentary image and constructed environment.[S1][S2]
