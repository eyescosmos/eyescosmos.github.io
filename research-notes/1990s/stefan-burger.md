# Stefan Burger

## 1. Basic facts
- German-born Swiss-based artist born in 1977, trained in photography in Zurich and active across photography, sculpture, installation, and action.[S1][S2][S3]
- In a photography-history context, Burger is relevant for using photographic illusion as a starting point for sculptural and conceptual investigations into representation, display, and audience expectation.[S1][S2][S3]

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: illusion, display, irony, institutional framing, subversive wit, and the unstable relation between image, object, and spectator.[S1][S2][S3]
- Technique / formal traits: photography-based works that spill into installation, wall-scale intervention, and sculptural construction; recurring attention to the medium’s capacity for illusion and to the theatrical conditions under which images are encountered.[S1][S2][S3]
- Representative work examples: the Kunstmuseum Stuttgart “Frischzelle” presentation is especially useful because it foregrounds Burger’s large wall-based intervention and frames his position explicitly as photographic while also making clear that photography in his practice often becomes the basis for spatial and conceptual transformation.[S1]
- Why this method was chosen: exhibition texts suggest that Burger is interested in the fact that photography is built for illusion. Rather than accepting that illusion as transparent seeing, he pushes it into awkward or humorous situations where the relation between artist, work, and viewer becomes newly visible.[S1][S2][S3]
- Historical context: Burger emerges in a period when many artists were testing the boundary between photography and installation. His work matters because it approaches this not as a smooth expansion of media, but as a problem of trick, display, and institutional expectation.[S1][S2][S3]
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: he can be related to conceptual photography and installation-based post-photographic practice, but his work is distinctive in the degree to which irony and material disruption accompany the photographic image.[S1][S2][S3]
- Historical significance: Burger matters because he shows that contemporary photography can no longer be understood only as flat image production; it is also a set of illusions that can be spatialized, mocked, and materially displaced.[S1][S2]
- Critical meaning: his work is important because it denies the viewer passive consumption of the image. The photographic field becomes a site where humor, deception, and institutional framing all operate together.[S1][S2][S3]
- Where and how the work was used: museum and foundation texts consistently present Burger through experimental exhibition formats that stress his movement between photography and other forms, which is exactly what makes him relevant for a broader history of the medium.[S1][S2][S3]

## 3. Criticism and reception
- Institutional reception repeatedly highlights Burger’s subtle irony and his challenge to the possibilities of photography as a medium of illusion.[S1][S3]
- Final website prose should emphasize that Burger’s importance lies in how he uses photography as a problem rather than as a stable object.[S1][S2]
- A strong critical line is that Burger turns photographic illusion into an event of spectatorship, where the viewer’s own expectations become part of the work.[S1][S2][S3]

### 8.1 本文改訂で強める論点
シュテファン・ブルガー（Stefan Burger）は、1990–2000年代の写真史で conceptual photography, exhibition critique と結びつけて整理したい作家である。活動地域・制度的文脈としては Switzerland を軸に、美術館、写真集、映像・インスタレーション、ギャラリー、批評誌、オンライン・アーカイブを通じて作品が流通した点を確認する必要がある。略歴だけではなく、display systems, studio, photographic production を、installation documentation, object-image relation, process-based work によってどのように写真の問題へ変えたのかを中心に読むと、本文に厚みが出る。

表現解説では、exhibition system, materiality, self-reflexivity を中心語にしつつ、作品を単なる代表作紹介で終わらせないことが重要である。1990–2000年代は、写真が紙のプリントだけでなく、映像、インスタレーション、出版、アーカイブ、ウェブ、ギャラリー制度、グローバル・ビエンナーレの中で再編された時期である。比較対象としては Peter Piller, Claude Closky を優先し、浅い類似ではなく、写真観、方法、対象の捉え方、受容史の差異を整理する。

評価・批評では、同時代の展覧会、写真集、ギャラリーでの流通、後年の美術館・研究機関による再評価を分けて扱う。展示、制作、写真の提示条件を作品化し、写真がどのように見せられるかを問う作家。 この一文を起点に、本文では「何を撮ったか」よりも「その方法が何を変えたか」を中心に組み立てる。
