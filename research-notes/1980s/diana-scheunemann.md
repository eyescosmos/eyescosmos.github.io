# Diana Scheunemann

## 1. Basic facts
- Swiss/German photographer who has lived and worked across Zürich, Paris, London, New York, Los Angeles, and Austin.[S1][S2]
- Known for editorial, celebrity, and autobiographical photography, especially the long-running self-portrait diary project *Behind My Face* begun in 1999.[S1]

## 2. Expression / method
- Main themes: self-exposure, lifestyle performance, celebrity portraiture, eroticism, freedom, and the unstable overlap between private diary and public image culture.[S1][S2]
- Representative work examples: the *Behind My Face* self-portrait diary, the feature-length documentary art film *Love American Skin* (2010), and her extensive portrait/editorial practice are central because they show how Scheunemann moves between commissioned image-making and autobiographical self-staging.[S1][S2]
- Technique / formal traits: diaristic self-portraiture, intimate snapshots expanded into project form, high-gloss editorial portraiture, and a willingness to blur lifestyle photography, fashion image, and personal document.[S1][S2]
- Why this method was chosen: Scheunemann’s own bio is especially useful here because it links the autobiographical work directly to her “exotic and, at times, extreme lifestyle.” The method seems chosen to keep photography close to lived experience and self-invention rather than to detached observation.[S1]
- Historical context: Scheunemann belongs to the turn-of-the-century moment when editorial photography, self-documentation, and autobiographical image culture were becoming increasingly intertwined. Her work sits at the edge of art, fashion, and media self-performance.[S1][S2]
- Relation to contemporaries or movements: she can be connected to diaristic self-portraiture and fashion/editorial photography, but differs from more conceptually framed self-portrait work by foregrounding immediacy, sexuality, and lifestyle as unstable performance.[S1][S2]
- Historical significance: Scheunemann matters insofar as she represents a strand of image practice in which the autobiographical self is constructed through the same visual economy as celebrity and fashion portraiture.[S1][S2]
- Critical meaning: the work matters because it makes it difficult to separate document from persona. Her self-portraits and portraits both treat photographic identity as something produced through circulation, style, and exposure.[S1][S2]
- Where and how the work was used: her photographs circulated widely through editorial commissions and portrait campaigns, while *Behind My Face* and *Love American Skin* extended that visual language into longer autobiographical form.[S1][S2]
