What Is Scientific Photography?

Scientific Photography refers to photography made for scientific purposes, such as the study of motion, astronomy, or medicine, and a source for later film and animation. This page places the term within the history of photography through representative photographers, nearby movements, and the periods in which the approach became visible.

Rather than stopping at a dictionary definition, this page places the term beside photographers, periods, and adjacent approaches so that its historical use can be read in context.

Where It Sits in the History of Photography

On this site, photographers connected to Scientific Photography appear mainly in 1870–1890s, often overlapping with Experimental Technique. The category is most useful when read alongside the people and contexts that made it visible.

Key Photographers
