Essay

Lee Miller (1907–1977) was an American photographer whose practice crossed Surrealist darkroom experiment, Vogue wartime photography, and Second World War frontline reporting. From solarization to the aftermath of Dachau, her work shows how composed images can also serve as historical evidence. Recent retrospectives have reassessed her as an independent photographer beyond her collaboration with Man Ray.
